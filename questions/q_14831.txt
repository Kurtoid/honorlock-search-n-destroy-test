According to Sigmund Freud, our personality develops from a conflict between two forces: our biological aggressive and pleasure-seeking drives versus our internal (socialized) control over these drives. Our personality is the result of our efforts to balance these two competing forces. Freud suggested that we can understand this by imagining three interacting systems within our minds. He called them the id, ego, and superego. The job of the ego, or self, is to balance the aggressive/pleasure-seeking drives of the id with the moral control of the superego.
