Which option is a common side effect of amoxicillin/clavulanic acid (Augmentin)? Correct Answer: GI distress Side effects of Augmentin are more common than with plain amoxicillin because both amoxicillin and clavulanic acid are involved. Augmentin is not as well tolerated by children because it does not taste as good as amoxicillin, and it tends to cause a much higher incidence of GI side effects (e.g., nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea) as well as vaginal candidiasis. GI side effects can be lessened by administering the drug prior to a meal.
